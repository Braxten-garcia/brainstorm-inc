Positioning Gaps

  • Hero says "for everyone" — a positioning gap that undermines the entire page. Hero
    The headline cycles through four audiences and the subheadline explicitly says "for everyone." BrainStorm's primary ICP is IT leaders at mid-to-large enterprises doing AI rollouts — the hero needs to choose one entry point. "For everyone" only works as positioning when you're already Salesforce.
  • AI is invisible on the homepage. Both
    BrainStorm's tagline is "Make AI work at work" and its #1 company priority is AI adoption — but "AI" appears nowhere in the hero and only in a footer content-pack link (Microsoft 365 Copilot). Every enterprise buyer is under pressure to show Copilot ROI right now. This tailwind is being left on the table.
  • The old way is never named. Both
    As a New Way product, BrainStorm needs to frame the painful status quo (fragmented LMS + email campaigns + manual tracking) before introducing itself as the replacement. The homepage jumps to features without establishing what's broken — visitors who aren't already problem-aware will miss why this matters.
  • Behavior change — the real differentiator — is buried. Full Page
    "BrainStorm has cracked the code on end-user behavior change" appears in section 1 body copy, and the Fishbowl stat (100-day go-live reduction, 14-point retention) proves it — but these are two-thirds down the page. This belongs in the hero or in section 1 as the lead claim.
  • Three-audience structure dilutes ICP focus. Full Page
    Software Vendors / Software Customers / Partners receive equal real estate with symmetrical use-case tabs. The homepage can't simultaneously be the best page for a software vendor CSM, an enterprise IT leader, and a channel reseller. The primary ICP should lead; the others should be clearly secondary paths.
  • "One-part LMS" undercuts the differentiation story. Full Page
    Describing BrainStorm as "one-part learning management system" positions it alongside the platforms it competes against (Docebo, Cornerstone). If BrainStorm is better than an LMS, don't use "LMS" to describe yourself — name the outcome instead (e.g., "one-part adaptive learning engine that actually changes behavior").
  • Testimonials and Awards sections dead-end. Full Page
    The testimonials carousel and awards block have no CTAs — missed conversion opportunities at two of the highest-credibility moments on the page. Even a "See how customers use BrainStorm →" or "Read case studies →" would keep visitors moving.

The Fix

Hero Fix

The hero needs to pick its primary audience — IT leaders doing AI rollouts — and lead with the problem they're hired to solve. "For everyone" must go. BrainStorm's own tagline ("Make AI work at work") and its #1 company priority are AI adoption, yet AI appears nowhere above the fold. The behavior-change angle is the real differentiator and the proof point (or at minimum a stat) should be visible without scrolling.

Suggested Headline
Make AI actually work at work.
Suggested Subheadline
BrainStorm drives real AI adoption — changing how employees work, not just giving them access to tools they don't use.

Full Page Fix

Restructure the audience section to lead with the primary ICP (IT / Enterprise AI Adoption) and make the vendor and partner paths clearly secondary. Then move the behavior-change proof — specifically the Fishbowl 100-day / 14-point stat and the Oracle CIO quote — into section 1 or 2, before the feature breakdown. These are BrainStorm's strongest credibility signals and they're currently buried two-thirds of the way down the page, long after most visitors have already left.
