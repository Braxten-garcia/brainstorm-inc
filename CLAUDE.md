## BrainStorm Brand & Voice

**What BrainStorm does:** Drives software adoption by working with organizations to change individual behavior. Main offering is the BrainStorm platform — helps organizations increase actual usage of software (e.g., lifting Microsoft Copilot adoption from 10% to 50%). Key differentiator: strategy + data. The platform measures how education impacts feature usage and retention, demonstrating ROI.

**Platform highlights:**
- **Flows** — modular, drag-and-drop learning flows (video, PDF, quizzes, etc.), easy to update on the fly
- **Analytics** — completion rates, engagement stats, in-line surveys, links training to product adoption metrics
- **Packs** — traditionally Microsoft-focused; expanding into AI productivity and security

**Target audience:**
- Primary: IT decision-makers and above
- Secondary: HR / learning management stakeholders

**Brand voice — the "Friendly" brand:**
- Personality words: Genuine, Clear, Expert, Relatable, Human
- Feel: Empowered, respected, understood, approachable
- Avoid: Stodgy, condescending, aggressive, overly technical (the "Willem Dafoe / Draco Malfoy" voice)
- Tone: Friendly, useful, quirky, confident, optimistic
- Brand role: Guide, advocate, trusted advisor, cheerleader
- Style: Short and sweet with a storytelling angle

**Language rules:**
- Use: "software adoption and usage"
- Avoid: business jargon; use "training" sparingly and tactfully
- Avoid: "deployment," "video platform"
- Style guide: BrainStorm stylebook, then Chicago style defaults
- Framing: Make the **customer** the hero of the story; speak to people like they're people — simple, emotional approach
