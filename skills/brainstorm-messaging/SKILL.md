## Brand narrative

### Elevator pitch

BrainStorm is the behavior-change engine for modern software adoption — turning AI access, security policy, and product capability into real-world usage, measurable outcomes, and scalable impact. We serve both the organizations deploying software to their people and the software vendors who need their customers to actually use what they've built.

### Positioning statement

For organizations investing in AI, security, and software innovation — and for the software vendors whose growth depends on customer adoption — BrainStorm is the behavior-change platform that turns access into adoption, so people change how they work and businesses realize measurable value. Unlike content libraries, LMS platforms, DAP tools, or one-time training providers, BrainStorm orchestrates sustained behavior change through multi-channel reinforcement, user personalization, and measurable adoption analytics.

### The problem we solve

Technology doesn't change work on its own. This is true whether you're an organization rolling out new tools to your employees, or a software vendor trying to get customers to adopt your product. In both cases, the pattern is the same: new tools get deployed, AI initiatives get launched, platforms get purchased — and then everyone wonders why outcomes don't change. Adoption stalls. Risk increases. And the value that looked obvious in a demo never shows up in day-to-day work.

Because the truth is simple: Work only changes when people do. People don't change because they attended a training or completed a course. They change when new behaviors are reinforced over time — inside real workflows, in the moments where decisions actually happen. And in today's world — where AI and security risks evolve weekly — that kind of change isn't optional anymore. It's infrastructure.

### Our solution

BrainStorm exists to make change real — for the people using software and for the vendors who build it. For enterprise organizations, we turn tool access into employee behavior change. For software vendors, we turn feature releases and product capabilities into real customer adoption. In both cases, we orchestrate behavior change at scale: meeting people where they work, reinforcing habits over time, and making adoption measurable — not just completed.

Because adoption isn't a launch. It's a system. And BrainStorm is the system that makes it happen.

## Value propositions

Six core value propositions mapped to the right audiences. Use these as the foundation for any BrainStorm messaging — express these ideas in context, don't paste them verbatim.

1. **Software value requires an aligned ecosystem** — End users must use software to benefit from it. Organizations must enable usage to see ROI. Vendors must help organizations prove that ROI fast enough to keep investing. End users are at the center of it all. *(All audiences)*

2. **Work only changes when people do** — Technology doesn't change work on its own. New tools don't create new outcomes. Work changes when people change what they do every day, in real situations. *(All audiences)*

3. **People drive both productivity and risk** — Powerful software in unenabled hands doesn't just underperform — it creates risk. When people aren't ready, every tool becomes a liability priced like a force multiplier. *(IT Leadership, Security & Compliance)*

4. **Change happens through habits, not training** — People don't change how they work because they attended a session or finished a course. Change happens when the right behaviors are reinforced over time, in the flow of real work. *(HR/L&D, IT Leadership)*

5. **If you can't see behavior change, it didn't happen** — Adoption isn't real unless it's visible. Leaders need to know whether people are engaging, changing habits, and using tools differently — not just completing assignments. *(IT Leadership, HR/L&D)*

6. **Change readiness is a core capability, not a project** — Organizations that treat adoption as a one-time initiative will always be catching up. In a world of constant technology change, the ability to move people forward continuously is what separates companies that scale from those that stall. *(All audiences)*

## The ADOPT™ framework

BrainStorm's proprietary framework for sustained adoption. Reference this when explaining BrainStorm's approach — it's a key differentiator and proof point.

- **A — Awareness:** Users understand that a change is needed.
- **D — Desire:** Users are motivated to explore the change.
- **O — Orientation:** Users learn how to make the change.
- **P — Participation:** Users actively begin using the change.
- **T — Transformation:** The change is sustained and becomes part of how work gets done.

The ADOPT™ framework guides, measures, and scales how people move from initial exposure to long-term behavior change and business impact. It's the broadest differentiator BrainStorm has and applies across all competitive categories.

## Target markets

### 1. AI adoption (including Microsoft 365 Copilot)

**Core pain:** AI access is deployed, but real usage and safe habits lag behind investment.

**BrainStorm's role:** Drives human behavior change that turns AI access into real impact; guides employees on how, when, and why to use AI; reinforces safe usage.

**Key messages:**
- AI adoption is business infrastructure
- AI access is not AI adoption
- Sustainable AI value comes from habits, not events
- AI security is the foundation of AI success

**Urgency triggers:** New AI initiative, Copilot/ChatGPT rollout, Board-level AI mandate, Low adoption post-deployment, Pressure to prove ROI on AI licensing

**Copilot-specific:** Behavior change turns Copilot access into sustained productivity gains and measurable AI ROI.

---

### 2. End-user security

**Core pain:** Employees fall for phishing, misuse credentials, make unsafe choices; traditional awareness training is one-time and disconnected from real risk.

**BrainStorm's role:** Combines proactive education with remedial training through Security Awareness and Threat Defense Packs.

**Key messages:**
- Human behavior is the primary attack surface
- Security training is security
- Prevention and remediation must work together
- Security that shames people fails

**Urgency triggers:** Security incident, Compliance audit, New regulatory requirement, Insurance review, Failed phishing simulation rates

---

### 3. Customer education (software vendors / SaaS)

**Core pain:** Vendors invest in onboarding and education, but customers still struggle to adopt key capabilities. Time-to-value is too slow, feature adoption stalls, renewal conversations happen before customers have experienced the product's full impact.

**BrainStorm's role:** Orchestrates behavior-driven customer enablement that moves users from access to habitual usage — making time-to-value faster, adoption visible, and education impact measurable against retention and growth.

**Key messages:**
- Adoption is the real measure of customer education
- Time-to-value is a behavior problem, not a content problem
- Customer education is a growth lever, not a cost center
- Customers who don't adopt don't renew

**Urgency triggers:** High churn, Low feature adoption, Slow time-to-value, Renewal pressure, Pressure to scale CS without adding headcount

## Writing with this skill

When drafting BrainStorm content, always:

- Lead with the problem (tools don't create outcomes on their own) before introducing BrainStorm
- Express the core belief: access is not adoption, behavior change requires reinforcement over time
- Reference the ADOPT™ framework when explaining BrainStorm's methodology
- Match value propositions to the audience's specific role and pain
- For competitive contexts, load the brainstorm-competitive skill alongside this one
- Apply visual brand rules from the brainstorm-brand skill for any designed output
- Never use "Content Packs" in copy — name the specific platform tools instead
- Never use em dashes — replace with commas, periods, or colons
- All headings in sentence case — only first word and proper nouns capitalized
