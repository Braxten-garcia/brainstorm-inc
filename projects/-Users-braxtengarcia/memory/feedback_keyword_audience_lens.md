When selecting target keywords for any page, always think through the lens of the specific person who would land on that page — their job title, their problem, and how they'd phrase a search. Don't pick keywords based on what BrainStorm calls the feature or use case internally.

**Why:** Keywords chosen from BrainStorm's internal framing often miss how buyers actually search, reducing organic discoverability for the right audience.

**How to apply:** Before picking a target keyword, identify the role (e.g., "Director of Customer Education at a SaaS company"). Then ask: what would that person type into Google when looking for this solution? That phrase — filtered through Semrush volume and difficulty data — should drive the keyword choice.
