## MARKETING ADVANTAGES

**BrainStorm** — a behavior-change platform that drives software adoption through personalized communications, learning, and analytics. ~$19M ARR, ~200 customers, growth stage.

## Overall Take

BrainStorm's real edge is the behavior-change angle — competitors measure clicks, BrainStorm measures whether people actually changed how they work. That's a harder claim to make but a more defensible one. The Microsoft referral channel and organic word-of-mouth are working quietly in the background, and the AI adoption wave is the best market timing they've had. The biggest untapped opportunity is surfacing proprietary adoption data externally to build category authority.

## Advantage Map

| # | Advantage | Strength Today | Potential | Priority |
|---|-----------|---------------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | **Behavior Change ROI** — measures whether employees actually changed how they work, not just whether they clicked a tooltip | Medium | High | **High** |
| 2 | **AI Adoption Tailwind** — every enterprise that bought Copilot/AI licenses is under pressure to show ROI right now | Medium | High | **High** |
| 3 | **Referral-Driven Distribution** — Microsoft introductions + customer-led word-of-mouth create warm pipeline competitors can't easily replicate | Medium | High | **High** |

## Where Advantages Compound

The tailwind creates urgency, the referral channel delivers warm introductions from trusted sources, and the behavior-change ROI story closes the deal. If BrainStorm surfaces its adoption data externally — a "State of AI Adoption" benchmark, enterprise adoption rates, what good looks like — it becomes the category authority that makes prospects seek them out before they even talk to sales.

## Advantages You Might Be Missing

- **Proprietary data asset:** BrainStorm has adoption behavior data across ~200 enterprise deployments. No competitor can publish that benchmark. An annual "State of AI Adoption" report would drive inbound, earn press, and create content that WalkMe and Whatfix literally cannot copy.

### Rationale

**Why Grow TOF with existing audience is the top priority:** The AI adoption wave is a forcing function — every enterprise that bought Copilot is under pressure to show ROI, and that's a new pool of buyers arriving constantly. Your existing ICP has far more headroom than you've captured.

**The connection to ICPs:** Lever 1 maps to Scaling ICPs (AI Adoption + Threat Defense) where 75% of marketing time is already going. Lever 4 (efficiency) helps those same ICPs convert faster. Lever 2 maps to PE portfolio (Testing, 5%) — present but not the priority.

**The trap to avoid:** Spreading content and campaign investment across too many audiences while trying to go all-in on AI adoption. The all-in bet only pays off with concentrated focus.

## BIG BET CAMPAIGNS

**BrainStorm** — a behavior-change platform that drives software adoption through personalized communications, learning, and analytics.

### Big Bet 1: The AI Adoption Gap Report

A proprietary annual benchmark using BrainStorm's platform data to quantify the widening gap between AI power users and everyone else. The asset no competitor can replicate — and the fuel that powers Big Bets 2 and 3.

- **Goal:**
  - Relevant advantages: Behavior Change ROI, AI Adoption Tailwind
  - Relevant high-level goals: Drive inbound pipeline from IT leaders; establish BrainStorm as the data authority on AI adoption
  - Measurement: Report downloads, MQLs generated, press placements earned, LinkedIn engagement on report content
- **Audience:**
  - Primary: IT Leaders at mid-to-large enterprises doing AI rollouts (Scaling ICP)
  - Secondary: IT Security/CISO leaders (Threat Defense)
- **Creative/Fuel:** Annual benchmark report using BrainStorm's internal usage data — showing real adoption rates, the gap between power users and the rest, and what behavior change looks like in practice. Key stat: what good AI adoption actually looks like across industries.
- **Channels/Engine:**
  - Primary: Inbound (gated report, PR placements, LinkedIn)
  - Supporting: Events (speaking fuel), Ecosystem (Microsoft CSM distribution)
- **Status:** Not tested as a campaign — but the data exists. The gap report format is proven in adjacent categories (State of DevOps, State of Marketing, etc.). Strong conviction.

---

### Big Bet 2: Category Authority at Events

A systematic campaign to stop attending events and start owning them — speaker slots, press coverage, awards, and thought leadership placements built around the AI adoption gap narrative. Turns the events you're already going to into category-defining moments.

- **Goal:**
  - Relevant advantages: AI Adoption Tailwind, Referral-Driven Distribution
  - Relevant high-level goals: B4 category authority; drive DCLs from events (50 DCLs targeted across Millenium Assembly, M365, Arizona MS, HIMSS)
  - Measurement: Speaking slots secured, press/digital placements, event DCLs vs. target, 10 G2 reviews in H1, industry awards submitted and won
- **Audience:**
  - Primary: IT Leaders at mid-to-large enterprises (Scaling ICP)
  - Secondary: IT Security/CISO leaders
- **Creative/Fuel:** Speaker pitches + thought leadership content built around "Access ≠ Adoption" and the power user gap narrative. The Gap Report (Big Bet 1) is the credibility anchor. Award submissions focused on AI adoption category.
- **Channels/Engine:**
  - Primary: Events (Millenium Assembly, M365 Conference, Arizona MS Event, HIMSS)
  - Supporting: PR/Ecosystem (digital placements, magazine articles, G2 reviews)
- **Status:** Partially tested — attending events already. Speaking/press/awards angle is new investment. Project Festival (Q1) is the first test of this motion.

---

### Big Bet 3: Microsoft Partner Activation

Create a content and asset layer that Microsoft CSMs can share proactively — so leads arrive at sales pre-warmed rather than cold. Turns the referral channel from a handoff into a pipeline accelerator.

- **Goal:**
  - Relevant advantages: Referral-Driven Distribution, Behavior Change ROI
  - Relevant high-level goals: Shorten sales cycle (B1 Velocity); increase DCLs from Microsoft channel
  - Measurement: % of Microsoft intros converting to sales meetings, time from intro to first sales call, DCLs attributed to Microsoft channel vs. baseline
- **Audience:**
  - Primary: IT Leaders at mid-to-large enterprises (Scaling ICP) — reached through Microsoft CSMs
- **Creative/Fuel:** Partner-ready content kit — the AI Adoption Gap Report + a co-branded one-pager + a short "AI Adoption Benchmark" summary Microsoft CSMs can share in an email intro.
- **Channels/Engine:**
  - Primary: Ecosystem (Microsoft partnership activation)
  - Supporting: Outbound (follow-up sequences after intro)
- **Status:** Not tested. Currently intros go direct to sales with no marketing layer. Low-cost to test — start with one CSM relationship and a single asset.

---

### How the Big Bets Connect

These three bets form a flywheel: the Gap Report (Bet 1) creates the proprietary data asset; Category Authority (Bet 2) distributes it through events and press to build awareness; Microsoft Partner Activation (Bet 3) uses it as the warm-up asset in the referral channel. Bet 1 is the foundation — prioritize it first because it fuels the other two.
